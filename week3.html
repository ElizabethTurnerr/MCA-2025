Week 3 - Encoding My Musical Score

Task Overview

Looking back on last week, I digitised my chosen piece with the tool MuseScore. This week I explored how we can make notated music accessible to people who might not have appropriate software, by converting the score into different symbolic encoding formats and then rendering it directly on a webpage.

This was my first time working with MusicXML, MEI and Verovio, so it definitely felt a little intimidating at first! However, once I figured out that each format simply stores musical information in a structured text form, in the same way HTML does for websites, it started to feel a bit more familiar. I am aware there can be a lot of issues surrounding this lab.

Files Exported

I exported my score to two important standards:

  • MusicXML - which I am familiar with
  • MEI
  • Moon River – MusicXML File
  • Moon River – MEI File

I then uploaded these files into my repository so they are stored and preserved as part of my dataset moving forward. Placing them in my dataset is like a safety blanket for me, and knowing I can refer back to them at any time is beneficial.
